Business & Revenue

Core SaaS business metrics and financial terms

ARR
Business

Annual Recurring Revenue

The predictable revenue a company expects to receive annually from subscriptions. The north star metric for SaaS businesses.

ARR = MRR × 12
MRR NRR
MRR
Business

Monthly Recurring Revenue

Normalized monthly revenue from all active subscriptions. The heartbeat of SaaS financial health.

MRR = Σ(Monthly Subscription Fees)
ARR Churn
CAC
Business

Customer Acquisition Cost

Total cost to acquire a new customer, including marketing, sales, and onboarding expenses.

CAC = Total Acquisition Cost / New Customers
LTV Payback
LTV
Business

Lifetime Value

Total revenue expected from a customer over their entire relationship. Healthy SaaS targets LTV:CAC ratio of 3:1+.

LTV = ARPU × Customer Lifetime
CAC ARPU
Churn
Business

Churn Rate

Percentage of customers or revenue lost over a period. The enemy of growth - reducing churn is often more valuable than acquiring new customers.

Churn = Lost Customers / Starting Customers × 100
NRR Retention
NRR
Business

Net Revenue Retention

Revenue retained from existing customers including expansions and contractions. NRR > 100% means growth without new customers.

NRR = (Start MRR + Expansion - Churn) / Start MRR
ARR Expansion
ARPU
Business

Average Revenue Per User

Average monthly revenue generated per active user or account. Key for pricing optimization and segment analysis.

ARPU = Total Revenue / Active Users
Payback
Business

CAC Payback Period

Months required to recover customer acquisition cost. Ideal payback is under 12 months for healthy unit economics.

Payback = CAC / (ARPU × Gross Margin)
Expansion
Business

Expansion Revenue

Additional revenue from existing customers through upsells, cross-sells, and seat additions. The most efficient growth lever.

Technical & Platform

Architecture, APIs, and infrastructure terms

API
Technical

Application Programming Interface

Set of protocols enabling software applications to communicate. REST and GraphQL are common API architectures in modern SaaS.

REST Webhook
REST
Technical

Representational State Transfer

Architectural style for web services using HTTP methods (GET, POST, PUT, DELETE) to manipulate resources.

Webhook
Technical

HTTP Callback

Automated HTTP POST sent when an event occurs. Enables real-time integrations without polling. Essential for payment and event-driven systems.

Multi-tenant
Technical

Multi-Tenancy Architecture

Single software instance serving multiple customers (tenants) with data isolation. The foundation of scalable SaaS economics.

RLS Isolation
Isolation
Technical

Tenant Isolation

Security boundary ensuring one customer cannot access another's data. Achieved via RLS policies, separate schemas, or separate databases.

RLS
Technical

Row-Level Security

Database security feature restricting data access at the row level based on policies. Critical for multi-tenant data isolation.

SaaS
Technical

Software as a Service

Cloud-based software delivery model with subscription pricing. Users access via browser without local installation.

SDK
Technical

Software Development Kit

Collection of tools, libraries, and documentation enabling developers to build applications for a specific platform.

OAuth
Technical

Open Authorization

Industry-standard protocol for token-based authorization. Enables "Login with Google/Meta" without sharing passwords.

JWT
Technical

JSON Web Token

Compact, URL-safe token format for securely transmitting claims between parties. Used for stateless authentication.

MFA
Technical

Multi-Factor Authentication

Security requiring multiple verification methods (password + TOTP/SMS). Essential for enterprise SaaS compliance.

SSO
Technical

Single Sign-On

Authentication allowing users to access multiple applications with one login. Common enterprise requirement using SAML or OIDC.

Marketing & Growth

Digital marketing and lead generation terms

Lead Gen
Marketing

Lead Generation

Process of attracting and capturing potential customers' contact information. The top of the sales funnel.

MQL SQL
Inbound
Marketing

Inbound Marketing

Strategy attracting customers through valuable content (blogs, SEO, social) rather than interruptive advertising.

CTA
Marketing

Call to Action

Prompt encouraging users to take a specific action. "Start Free Trial" and "Book Demo" are common SaaS CTAs.

MQL
Marketing

Marketing Qualified Lead

Lead meeting marketing criteria for engagement level and fit. Ready for sales nurturing but not yet sales-ready.

SQL PQL
SQL
Marketing

Sales Qualified Lead

Lead vetted by sales as having budget, authority, need, and timeline (BANT). Ready for direct sales engagement.

PQL
Marketing

Product Qualified Lead

Lead showing buying intent through product usage (trial engagement, feature adoption). The holy grail of PLG motion.

PLG
Marketing

Product-Led Growth

Go-to-market strategy where the product itself drives acquisition, conversion, and expansion. Enables viral growth loops.

CTR
Marketing

Click-Through Rate

Percentage of impressions resulting in clicks. Key metric for ad and email performance.

CTR = Clicks / Impressions × 100
CVR
Marketing

Conversion Rate

Percentage of visitors completing a desired action (signup, purchase). The ultimate marketing efficiency metric.

CVR = Conversions / Visitors × 100
ROAS
Marketing

Return on Ad Spend

Revenue generated per dollar spent on advertising. ROAS of 3x means $3 revenue for every $1 spent.

ROAS = Revenue from Ads / Ad Spend
CPC
Marketing

Cost Per Click

Average cost paid for each click on an advertisement. Primary pricing model for search and social ads.

CPC = Total Ad Spend / Total Clicks
CPM
Marketing

Cost Per Mille (Thousand)

Cost per 1,000 ad impressions. Standard pricing for display and video advertising.

CPM = (Cost / Impressions) × 1000

AI & Machine Learning

Artificial intelligence and predictive analytics terms

ML
AI

Machine Learning

AI subset where systems learn from data to improve predictions without explicit programming. Powers recommendations, predictions, and automation.

SHAP
AI

SHapley Additive exPlanations

Method explaining ML predictions by quantifying each feature's contribution. Essential for AI transparency and trust.

RFM
AI

Recency, Frequency, Monetary

Customer segmentation model based on purchase behavior. Classifies customers into segments like Champions, At-Risk, Lost.

Trust Gate
AI

Trust Gate

Decision checkpoint that evaluates signal health before allowing automation execution. Pass/Hold/Block based on data quality thresholds.

Signal Health Autopilot
Signal Health
AI

Signal Health Score

Composite score (0-100) measuring data reliability. Components: EMQ (40%), Freshness (25%), Variance (20%), Anomalies (15%).

Autopilot
AI

Trust-Gated Autopilot

Automated execution system that only acts when signal health passes safety thresholds. Never auto-executes when health < 70.

EMQ
AI

Event Match Quality

Score measuring how well events match between your system and ad platforms. Higher EMQ = better attribution accuracy.

A/B Test
AI

A/B Testing

Controlled experiment comparing two variants to determine which performs better. Statistical significance required for valid conclusions.

Bayesian
AI

Bayesian Statistics

Statistical approach expressing results as probabilities. "95% probability B is better" is more intuitive than p-values.

Metrics & Analytics

Performance measurement and reporting terms

KPI
Metrics

Key Performance Indicator

Measurable value demonstrating effectiveness in achieving objectives. Good KPIs are specific, measurable, and actionable.

North Star
Metrics

North Star Metric

Single metric that best captures core value delivered to customers. Aligns entire company around one measurable goal.

DAU/MAU
Metrics

Daily/Monthly Active Users

Unique users engaging with product daily/monthly. DAU/MAU ratio measures product stickiness (20%+ is strong).

Attribution
Metrics

Marketing Attribution

Determining which touchpoints contribute to conversions. Models: First-touch, Last-touch, Linear, Time-decay, Position-based, Data-driven.

MTA
Metrics

Multi-Touch Attribution

Attribution model crediting multiple touchpoints in the customer journey. More accurate than single-touch models.

Cohort
Metrics

Cohort Analysis

Grouping users by shared characteristics (signup date, acquisition channel) to analyze behavior patterns over time.

Customer & CDP

Customer data platform and lifecycle terms

CDP
Customer

Customer Data Platform

Unified system collecting, organizing, and activating customer data from all sources. Creates single customer view for personalization.

Identity Segment
Identity
Customer

Identity Resolution

Process of connecting multiple identifiers (email, device ID, phone) to a single customer profile. Enables cross-device tracking.

Segment
Customer

Customer Segment

Group of customers sharing common characteristics. Used for targeted marketing, personalization, and audience syncing to ad platforms.

Retention
Customer

Customer Retention

Ability to keep customers over time. The inverse of churn. 5% retention improvement can increase profits 25-95%.

Retention = 1 - Churn Rate
NPS
Customer

Net Promoter Score

Customer loyalty metric based on "How likely to recommend?" (0-10). Promoters (9-10) minus Detractors (0-6).

NPS = %Promoters - %Detractors
CSAT
Customer

Customer Satisfaction Score

Direct measure of customer satisfaction with a product, service, or interaction. Usually 1-5 scale after key touchpoints.

TTV
Customer

Time to Value

Duration from signup to achieving first meaningful outcome. Shorter TTV correlates with higher retention and conversion.

Audience Sync
Customer

Audience Sync

Pushing CDP segments to ad platforms (Meta, Google, TikTok, Snapchat) for targeting. Uses hashed identifiers for privacy.

CAPI
Customer

Conversions API

Server-side event tracking bypassing browser limitations. Essential for accurate attribution in cookie-less future.
