## Design Context

### Users
Marketing agencies managing multiple client accounts across ad platforms. They need efficiency, clear performance insights, and reliable automation across many brands. They're in the tool frequently — checking trust scores, reviewing autopilot decisions, syncing audiences, and generating client reports. Context: professional setting, often multi-monitor, extended sessions.

### Brand Personality
**Bold, intelligent, premium.** High-end SaaS energy — feels like Bloomberg terminal meets luxury brand. Sophisticated power. The interface communicates competence through restraint, not flash. Every element earns its place.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Reference**: Apple / Porsche Design — luxury product design with premium materials feel, restrained palette, obsessive attention to detail
- **Anti-reference**: Enterprise gray (Salesforce/SAP style — gray, dense, cluttered, feels like work rather than a tool you want to use)
- **Theme**: Dark mode primary (agency users in extended sessions, data-heavy dashboards). Light mode for marketing/landing pages.
- **Palette**: Deep, rich darks with warm coral/red accent (#FF4D4D). Neutrals tinted toward the brand hue. Accent used sparingly for maximum impact — 60/30/10 rule.
- **Typography**: Clash Display (display/headings) + Satoshi (body). Both geometric, both premium, neither overused. No monospace-as-aesthetic.

### Design Principles
1. **Quiet authority** — The interface communicates power through precision, not noise. Restrained palette, intentional spacing, no gratuitous decoration.
2. **Information density without clutter** — Agency users need data. Present it clearly with strong hierarchy, not by hiding it behind progressive disclosure.
3. **Premium materiality** — Every surface, border, and shadow should feel considered. Tinted neutrals, not gray. Subtle depth, not flat.
4. **Decisive contrast** — Bold typographic hierarchy with at least 1.5x ratio between scale steps. The eye knows exactly where to go.
5. **Earn every pixel** — If an element doesn't serve the user's task, remove it. Decorative elements must reinforce brand, never distract.
